RESEARCH PURPOSE
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Title: Development and Validation of a Non-Invasive Urinary Biomarker
       Classifier for Early Sepsis Detection (SepsisDx)

Purpose:

We are developing SepsisDx, a machine learning-based clinical decision
support tool that uses non-invasive urinary biomarkers — specifically
C-reactive protein normalized to creatinine (CRP/Cr) and interferon
gamma-induced protein 10 normalized to creatinine (IP-10/Cr) — to predict
sepsis in hospitalized patients. The system is designed to provide rapid,
point-of-care screening using urine ELISA assays, complementing existing
blood-based diagnostic methods.

We are requesting access to the MIMIC-IV database to:

1. Extract serum CRP (itemid 50889, 51652) and urine creatinine
   (itemid 51082, 51106, 52000) measurements from ICU patients, linked
   to Sepsis-3 diagnoses (ICD-9/10 codes), to construct a real-world
   validation dataset for our CRP/Creatinine-ratio-based classifier.

2. Characterize the distribution of CRP and creatinine values across
   septic vs. non-septic ICU populations to refine the decision
   thresholds used by our algorithm, which were originally derived from
   a small reference cohort (n=6) and synthetic data.

3. Benchmark our lightweight urinary biomarker model against the richer
   clinical feature sets available in MIMIC-IV (vitals, labs, SOFA scores)
   to quantify the diagnostic trade-off between a simple 2-biomarker
   urine test and comprehensive ICU monitoring data.

4. Evaluate the generalizability of published urinary biomarker panels
   (Su et al., Sci Rep 2021; Bhatt & Das, Pediatr Res 2021) by comparing
   their reported CRP/Cr distributions against CRP and creatinine
   measurements observed in the MIMIC-IV population.

This research is strictly observational and retrospective. No patient
contact or intervention is involved. All analyses will be performed on
de-identified data. Results will be used to improve the accuracy and
clinical utility of the SepsisDx software tool.
