User Manual

Complete documentation for the SepsisDx ML classification system.

Important Clinical Disclaimer

This tool is a clinical decision support system, not a standalone diagnostic. Results must always be interpreted in the context of the full clinical picture, including patient history, physical examination, vital signs, and other laboratory findings.

The system is validated against simulated datasets modeled on published urine ELISA biomarker distributions. Performance metrics (AUROC, sensitivity, specificity) reflect this validation data and may differ in real-world clinical populations. Always correlate with blood culture and other diagnostic workup.

Do not withhold empiric antibiotics or delay clinical intervention based solely on a "Not Septic" classification. A negative result should lower suspicion but does not rule out sepsis.

Introduction

SepsisDx is a machine learning system for detecting sepsis from urine ELISA biomarker measurements. It uses two urinary biomarker ratios — CRP/Creatinine and IP-10/Creatinine — to classify patients as septic or non-septic.

The system is validated against the blood-based gold standard: positive blood culture combined with Sepsis-3 criteria (SOFA score ≥ 2).

Why Urine?

Traditional sepsis detection relies on blood draws and lab cultures, which are invasive and slow. Urine-based ELISA testing offers a non-invasive, rapid alternative. This software improves on the existing rule-based SepsisDx classifier by using machine learning to catch borderline cases that hard-threshold rules miss.

Clinical Workflow

The recommended workflow for integrating SepsisDx into clinical practice:

1

Collect Urine Sample

Obtain a clean-catch urine specimen from the patient. Run the ELISA assay to measure CRP, IP-10, and creatinine concentrations. Calculate the CRP/Creatinine and IP-10/Creatinine ratios in pg/mg.

2

Enter Biomarker Values

Navigate to Predict and enter the two biomarker ratios. For multiple patients, use Batch to upload a CSV file.

3

Review the Classification

The system returns a prediction (Septic / Not Septic), a probability score, a risk level (LOW to VERY HIGH), and a comparison with the rule-based SepsisDx result. Review the SHAP contribution breakdown to understand which biomarker drove the prediction.

4

Correlate with Clinical Context

Integrate the result with the patient's clinical presentation, vital signs, CBC/differential, lactate, procalcitonin, and blood culture status. The ML prediction should support — not replace — clinical judgment.

5

Act on the Result

Use the risk level and probability to guide urgency. HIGH and VERY HIGH results warrant prompt evaluation for the Sepsis-3 bundle (blood cultures, lactate, empiric antibiotics within 1 hour). LOW results may help de-escalate workup in patients with low pre-test probability.

Interpreting Results

Understanding the Probability Score

The probability score (0.0 – 1.0) represents the model's confidence that the patient has sepsis. This is not the same as the prevalence or pre-test probability. A well-calibrated model means that if 100 patients each receive a 70% probability, approximately 70 of them will truly have sepsis. You can verify model calibration on the Explain page (Calibration tab).

When the ML and SepsisDx Disagree

The prediction page highlights cases where the ML model and the rule-based SepsisDx system reach different conclusions. These disagreements are clinically important:

  • ML positive, SepsisDx negative: The patient's biomarkers fall below the hard thresholds (CRP/Cr ≤ 300 or IP-10/Cr ≤ 100) but the ML model detects a pattern consistent with sepsis. These are potential borderline cases that rule-based systems miss. Pursue further workup.
  • ML negative, SepsisDx positive: One biomarker exceeds its threshold, but the overall pattern is more consistent with SIRS or non-infectious inflammation. Consider whether the clinical picture supports a non-infectious cause.

Reading the SHAP Explanation

Each single-patient prediction includes a SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) breakdown. This tells you why the model made its decision:

Positive SHAP Value

This biomarker increased the sepsis probability. The larger the value, the stronger the push toward a "Septic" classification.

Negative SHAP Value

This biomarker decreased the sepsis probability. The larger the magnitude, the stronger the push toward "Not Septic."

For example, a patient with CRP/Cr = 299 and IP-10/Cr = 80 may show IP-10/Cr with a positive SHAP value (pushing toward sepsis) even though it is below the rule-based threshold of 100. This is because the ML model has learned from training data that values in this range carry meaningful clinical risk, unlike the rigid threshold-based system.

Clinical Action by Risk Level

Risk Level Probability Suggested Clinical Action
LOW < 20% Sepsis unlikely. Continue clinical observation. Consider alternative diagnoses for presenting symptoms. Standard monitoring.
MODERATE 20% – 50% Borderline result. Obtain blood cultures if not already drawn. Consider serial monitoring with repeat biomarkers. Assess SOFA score components.
HIGH 50% – 80% Sepsis probable. Initiate Sepsis-3 bundle: draw blood cultures, measure lactate, begin empiric antibiotics within 1 hour. Monitor for organ dysfunction.
VERY HIGH > 80% Sepsis highly likely. Urgent Sepsis-3 bundle. Consider ICU-level care. Aggressive fluid resuscitation, vasopressors if needed. Source control evaluation.

These are suggested actions only. Always apply clinical judgment based on the full patient presentation.

Limitations to Be Aware Of

  • Urine quality matters. Contaminated or highly dilute specimens may produce unreliable biomarker ratios. If creatinine is unusually low, consider repeat collection.
  • Non-infectious inflammation. CRP elevation occurs in many conditions (surgery, trauma, autoimmune flares). The ML model is better than rule-based thresholds at distinguishing SIRS from sepsis, but some overlap remains.
  • Immunocompromised patients. Patients on immunosuppressants, with neutropenia, or on chronic steroids may have blunted biomarker responses. A LOW risk score does not exclude sepsis in these populations.
  • Pediatric considerations. If using data from the pediatric population profile, note that normal creatinine ranges differ from adults. Models trained on adult populations may not generalize to children.
  • Model currency. Models should be retrained periodically as new validated data becomes available. Check the model's export date on the Models page.

Biomarkers

Biomarker Unit SepsisDx Threshold Description
CRP / Creatinine pg/mg > 300 → Septic C-Reactive Protein normalized by urinary creatinine
IP-10 / Creatinine pg/mg > 100 → Septic Interferon gamma-induced Protein 10 (CXCL10) normalized by creatinine

Values are derived from urine ELISA assays. Creatinine normalization corrects for urine dilution.

Classifiers

SepsisDx Baseline

Rule-Based

CRP/Cr > 300 OR IP-10/Cr > 100 → Septic. Hard thresholds miss borderline cases like Sample 6 (CRP/Cr=299, IP-10/Cr=80).

Logistic Regression

ML

L2-regularized with internal feature scaling. Fast, well-calibrated probabilities. Good baseline ML model.

Random Forest

Ensemble

200 decision trees (max depth 5). Handles non-linear boundaries. Typically the best overall performer.

XGBoost

Gradient Boosting

200 estimators with automatic class imbalance handling. Uses all 7 features (basic + engineered).

Neural Network

Deep Learning

MLP architecture: 32 → 16 → 8 → output. ReLU activation, Adam optimizer, early stopping, minority class oversampling. Best calibration (lowest ECE).

Training Models

The training pipeline runs the following steps:

  1. Data Loading — loads the selected dataset
  2. Cross-Validation — stratified 5-fold CV for all selected classifiers
  3. Threshold Optimization — finds optimal classification thresholds
  4. Model Export — trains on full dataset, exports as .pkl with JSON metadata

Note: Training runs in the background. You can monitor progress in real time via the progress page. Training on 5,000 samples typically takes 30–60 seconds.

SHAP Explainability

SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) values show how each biomarker contributes to individual predictions. This is critical for clinical trust — clinicians need to understand why a model makes a prediction.

Positive SHAP Value

Feature pushes prediction toward "Septic"

Negative SHAP Value

Feature pushes prediction toward "Not Septic"

Example: Borderline Sample 6 (Septic)

CRP/Cr = 299.0, IP-10/Cr = 80.0

  • IP-10/Cr = 80.0 → +0.5922 (increases sepsis risk)
  • CRP/Cr = 299.0 → -0.0475 (slightly decreases risk)

The ML model learned that IP-10/Cr = 80 is clinically significant, unlike the hard threshold rule (which requires > 100).

Threshold Optimization

max_f1Maximize F1 scoreBalanced precision/sensitivity

max_youdenMaximize Youden's JOptimal balance (default)

Glossary

Term Definition

CRPC-Reactive Protein, an acute-phase inflammatory biomarker
ECEExpected Calibration Error — how well predicted probabilities match actual outcomes

  4. Wu Y, Wang G, Huang Z, et al. Diagnostic and therapeutic value of biomarkers in urosepsis. Therapeutic Advances in Urology. 2023;15. — Reviews urinary CRP, procalcitonin, and novel markers for urosepsis.
